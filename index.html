What is LIVRE OS?

LIVRE OS is an identity-native operating system. Instead of sending documents and personal data to every app, you keep everything inside Solivre, your sovereign identity kernel, and share only cryptographic proofs.

Solivre Kernel

Your identity engine. Manages claims, permissions, proof descriptors and key lifecycle without ever exposing raw data on-chain.

LivreID

Human-readable identity names like odin.livre that resolve to private identity anchors instead of public profiles.

LivreVault

Encrypted storage for documents, certificates, and metadata, hashed and verifiable, but unreadable to anyone except you.

How it works

1

Create your Solivre identity

Generate keys, bind your claims and choose a LivreID name. Your identity is represented as cryptographic commitments, not a public profile.

2

Store documents privately

Upload documents into LivreVault. Everything is encrypted locally; only hashes are committed on-chain for integrity checks.

3

Approve proof requests

When an app or institution needs to verify something (like age or residency), you receive a clear, human-readable request inside LIVRE.

4

Private computation on COTI

LivreNodes run garbled circuits / MPC over your commitments, outputting only a boolean result and proof hash.

5

They get truth, not data

The requester receives a verifiable yes/no answer with a timestamp, no personal data, no documents, no long-term storage.

Architecture at a glance

LIVRE OS is built as a layered system, from COTI's gcEVM base to the apps and institutions that rely on private proofs.

Layers

  • Layer 5 - Apps & Institutions
  • Layer 4 - LivreKit SDK & APIs
  • Layer 3 - LivreID, Proof Engine, LivreVault
  • Layer 2 - Solivre Kernel
  • Layer 1 - LIVRE Core Contracts
  • Layer 0 - COTI gcEVM Network

Design principles

  • Zero raw identity on-chain
  • User-owned keys and storage
  • Minimal, non-linkable proofs
  • Composable claims and policies
  • Auditable contracts and circuits
