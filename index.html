What is LIVRE OS?

LIVRE OS is an identity-native operating system. Instead of sending documents and personal data to every app, you keep everything inside Solivre — your sovereign identity kernel — and share only cryptographic proofs.

Solivre Kernel

Your identity engine. Manages claims, permissions, proof descriptors and key lifecycle without ever exposing raw data on-chain.

LivreID

Human-readable identity names like odin.livre that resolve to private identity anchors instead of public profiles.

LivreVault

Encrypted storage for documents, certificates, and metadata – hashed and verifiable, but unreadable to anyone except you.

How it works

Create your Solivre identity

Generate keys, bind your claims and choose a LivreID name. Your identity is represented as cryptographic commitments, not a public profile.

Approve proof requests

Apps and institutions ask for proofs (like age or residency). You approve or deny every request from within your wallet.

Private computation on COTI

LivreNodes run garbled circuits and MPC on your commitments, returning only a boolean result and proof hash.

Who LIVRE OS is for

Individuals

Keep your documents, claims, and identity under your control. Share proofs instead of files. Reduce risk of breaches and identity theft.

Companies & Institutions

Get compliant yes/no answers without storing sensitive user data. Lower your liability and simplify onboarding.

Developers

Integrate LIVRE OS through LivreKit SDK and APIs. Ask for proofs, not documents. Build apps on top of a sovereign identity layer.
